This software is offered under the [PolyForm Noncommercial License 1.0.0](LICENSE) for noncommercial use. **Commercial use requires a separate commercial license.**

## What counts as noncommercial use (free under the LICENSE)

You may use this software at no cost for:

- Personal use, hobby projects, and amateur pursuits
- Research, experiment, and testing for the benefit of public knowledge
- Use by any **charitable organization, educational institution, public research organization, public safety or health organization, environmental protection organization, or government institution** -- regardless of the source of funding
- Open-source projects that are themselves noncommercial

University researchers, non-profit labs, students, hobbyists, and government scientists can use this software freely without contacting the author.

## What requires a commercial license

You need a commercial license if you (or the entity you work for) want to use this software for any of the following:

- **Internal use at a for-profit company** (including biotech, pharma, contract research organizations, consulting firms)
- Building a **product or service** (including SaaS) that uses this software, where customers pay you
- **Consulting engagements** where you charge clients to set up, run, or operate this software for them
- Any other use that has anticipated commercial application

## How to obtain a commercial license

Contact the maintainer privately through the repository profile or project homepage with:

1. **Company name** and a brief description of your organization
2. **Intended use case** -- what you want to build or run
3. **Approximate scale** -- number of seats / users / projects, deployment model
4. **Preferred contact** for follow-up

A typical commercial license includes:

- Right to use the software for the specified commercial purpose
- Right to make modifications for internal use
- Annual term with renewal option
- Pricing scaled to the size of the organization and scope of use

Discounted or free terms are available for early-stage startups and small businesses on a case-by-case basis.

## Why dual licensing?

This project is built and maintained primarily as a research tool. Dual licensing keeps it freely available to the academic and non-profit research community while providing a sustainable path for ongoing development funded by commercial users who derive economic value from it.
